Welcome to your MSc

  • designed as a solid research training

  • for people interested in the detail of the (sub)discipline

  • (unashamedly) heavy on methods

180 Credits of MSc

Coursework

  • MScs involve a lot of independent study

  • most courses have a break in week 6

  • courses have more than one assessment

  • if you need an extension, speak to the postgraduate office

Dissertation

  • worth thinking about early

    • indicative topics released in October
    • talk to potential supervisors October/November
    • poster day with initial ideas, most likely December

  • students can work in pairs (writeup is always independent)

  • talk to several potential supervisors!

  • funding: apply to postgraduate office, talk to your PD first

  • funding isn’t huge, maybe around £400 tops

Marking

our marking scale

  • 40 is a (bare) pass

  • 50 is MSc-level performance

  • 70 is a high mark (“distinction”)

you’re aiming for an average of >50 on coursework, and >50 on the dissertation
