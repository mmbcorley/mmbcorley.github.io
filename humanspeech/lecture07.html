The Motor Theory of Speech Perception

  1. speech processing is special (unique to humans)

  2. perceiving speech is perceiving the vocal gestures of the speaker

  3. the motor system is necessary for perceiving speech

  • chinchillas can discriminate speech sounds

(Kuhl & Miller, 1975)

Liberman & Mattingly (1985)

  • aversion conditioning

  • also, babies can discriminate sounds they’ve never made

A Little Motor Evidence

Möttönen & Watkins (2012)

  • motor cortex must be involved in speech perception

  • TMS was applied to the left M1 cortex, lip area

  • post-TMS, proportion of “different” responses reduced where lip involvement was the discriminating factor (e.g., /pa-ta/)
