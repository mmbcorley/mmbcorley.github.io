The Machinery of Speech

The Vocal Tract

  • series of cavities in the body that are interconnected

  • lungs are “power source” for (most) speech

    • one sign of drowning is not being able to talk
    • driven by diaphragm

  • trachea

    • lungs join mouth and nose
    • also breathing in
    • food gets diverted to esophagus by epiglottis

  • larynx buzzes (not always, e.g., hissing)

    • air always passes through

  • mouth and nose filter, amplify, change speech sounds

    • end of process at lips (and nostrils)
